Research


Current Research


Computational Atomic Physics, Ultrafast Electronic Dynamics
PI: Klaus Bartschat, Ph.D.
Drake University

The goal of our research is to study the effects of the Carrier Phase Envelope (CEP) of femtosecond laser pulses on the ionization of atomic Hydrogen. Using Fortran, OpenMP, and XSEDE Supercomputing resources such as Stampede, I develop job scripts and preform numerical analysis on data. In October, I was able to attend the Frontiers In Optics (FOI) 2014 National Conference in Tucson, AZ to present our findings.


Computational Bioinfromatics, Automating NRM assignment
PI: Timothy Urness, Ph.D.
Drake University

We are looking to accelerate the non-trivial assignment of nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) datasets. We have used a search based algorithm. In order to reduce assignment time, we have recently implemented machine learning to limit the branching factor. On this project, I have developed this algorithm from the ground up with a group of 5 colleagues. This includes unit testing, algorithmic improvements, and optimizing our algorithm. I have presented this research at local and regional conferences.


Past Research


American Mathematical Society (AMS), Sage Math
PI: Jason Grout, Ph.D.
Drake University

The goal of this research was to develop a three.js based 3D graphics for the Sage Cell Server. Throughout the project, I worked on developing and testing the pythreejs platform. I also trained new researchers as they joined the project. This included bringing them up to speed with the code base and helping them get going on their first project.


Signal and Image Analysis, MCTP Pre-REU
PI: Gregory Berkolaiko, Ph.D.
Texas A&M Uinversity

The goal of this project was to develop fraud protection software. We worked on developing and implementing a signature authentication algorithm. We used Fourier analysis and Wavelet Decomposition to analyze the signatures. We then compared them to our database to identify forgeries. Over 80 signatures were tested with a 85% success rate.
